Corporate Water Stress Financial Analysis

Quantifying financial materiality of water risk by combining corporate water usage data with regional stress indices for sector-level investment analysis

🐍 Python & R📊 Risk Scoring & Scenarios📅 Multi-sector analysis💧 WRI Aqueduct framework

1. Research Question & Motivation

"Which sectors face the highest financial risk from water stress, and how material is this risk under forward-looking water scarcity scenarios?"

Water is the 'next carbon' — an underpriced natural resource that is becoming increasingly scarce. Unlike carbon, water risk is highly localised: the same factory design faces vastly different water risk depending on whether it's built in Finland or India. Companies that are both water-intensive and located in high-stress basins face potential production shutdowns, stranded assets, and regulatory action.

2. Data Collection & Sources

Water Usage
Simulated corporate water withdrawal data modelled on CDP Water disclosures (m³ per year)
Stress Index
WRI Aqueduct regional water stress scores (0–5 scale: Low, Low-Medium, Medium-High, High, Extremely High)
Financial Data
Revenue, sector classification, facility locations
Metric
Water intensity = water withdrawal (m³) / revenue ($M) — normalises usage by firm size
Scenarios
WRI projections for 2030 and 2040 under optimistic, business-as-usual, and pessimistic pathways
import pandas as pd

# Load water risk data
water = pd.read_csv('corporate_water_risk.csv')

# Compute water intensity
water['water_intensity'] = water['water_withdrawal_m3'] / water['revenue_millions']

# Financial materiality score = intensity × regional stress
water['materiality'] = water['water_intensity'] * water['stress_index']

# Sector summary
print(water.groupby('sector')[['water_intensity', 'materiality']].mean()
      .sort_values('materiality', ascending=False))

3a. Water Risk Scoring Framework

Combines three dimensions: (1) water intensity (usage per $ revenue), (2) regional water stress (WRI Aqueduct), and (3) water dependency (how critical water is to the production process). The product gives financial materiality.

# Three-factor water risk score
water['risk_score'] = (
    water['water_intensity_norm'] *    # How much water they use
    water['stress_index_norm'] *       # How scarce water is locally
    water['dependency_score']          # How critical water is to operations
)

# Revenue at risk
water['revenue_at_risk_pct'] = water['risk_score'] * 100  # rough estimate

5a. Forward-Looking Scenario Analysis

Models revenue impact under three WRI water stress trajectories (optimistic, BAU, pessimistic) to 2040. Stress-tests portfolio-level financial exposure to water scarcity.

# Scenario analysis
scenarios = {
    'optimistic': 1.1,   # 10% stress increase by 2040
    'bau': 1.3,          # 30% increase
    'pessimistic': 1.6   # 60% increase
}

for name, multiplier in scenarios.items():
    future_risk = water['risk_score'] * multiplier
    revenue_at_risk = (future_risk * water['revenue_millions']).sum()
    print(f"{name}: Total revenue at risk = ${revenue_at_risk:,.0f}M")

Key Findings

1
Agriculture and Utilities show 3–5× higher water stress exposure than the median sector, driven by both high water intensity and location in stressed basins
2
Semiconductor manufacturing faces critical water risk despite lower absolute volumes — ultra-pure water requirements make production highly sensitive to supply disruption
3
Under pessimistic water stress scenarios (2040), the most exposed portfolio quintile faces an estimated 12–18% of revenue at risk from water scarcity
⚠️ Methodological Caveat

Water risk is hyper-local — facility-level analysis is far more accurate than country or company-level averages. Ground truth data on water withdrawal is scarce; most corporate water disclosures are voluntary and may understate consumption. Cross-reference with satellite data where possible.
