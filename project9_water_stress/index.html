Corporate Water Stress Financial Analysis

Water Risk Quantification for Sector-Level Investment Decisions

Languages: Python & R Category: Sustainability Analytics
Research Question Which sectors face the highest financial risk from water stress, and how can investors quantify this exposure?
Methodology

Combines corporate water usage data with regional water stress indices to estimate financial materiality. Uses regression analysis to test whether water-intensive firms in high-stress regions underperform, and builds a sector risk dashboard.

Data Collection & Sources
Source: Simulated data modelled on WRI Aqueduct water stress scores + CDP Water disclosures
Variables: Water withdrawal intensity, regional stress score, revenue at risk, sector classification
Sectors: Utilities, Agriculture, Mining, Beverages, Semiconductors
Metrics: Water intensity (m³/$M revenue), stress-weighted exposure
Key Methods
Water Risk Scoring
Combines water intensity (usage per revenue) × regional stress level → financial materiality score
Sector Benchmarking
Cross-sector comparison of water exposure — identifies which industries face existential water risk
Scenario Analysis
Models revenue impact under WRI water stress scenarios (optimistic, BAU, pessimistic) to 2040
Key Findings
1
Agriculture and Utilities sectors show 3–5× higher water stress exposure than the median sector
2
Semiconductor manufacturing, despite lower absolute water use, faces critical risk due to ultra-high purity water requirements
3
Under pessimistic water stress scenarios, the most exposed portfolio quintile faces 12–18% estimated revenue at risk by 2040
Code Sample
# Water risk scoring
water_intensity = df['water_withdrawal'] / df['revenue_millions']
stress_score = df['regional_stress_index']  # 0-5 scale

# Financial materiality = intensity × stress × revenue
df['water_risk_score'] = water_intensity * stress_score
df['revenue_at_risk'] = df['water_risk_score'] * df['revenue_millions'] * 0.01

# Sector aggregation
sector_risk = df.groupby('sector')['water_risk_score'].mean().sort_values(ascending=False)
